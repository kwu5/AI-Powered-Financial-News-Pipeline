## Major Market Movements
- Oil prices fell sharply on hopes of a U.S.-Iran peace deal, with Brent crude dropping 5.5% to around $97.70 a barrel (per BBC).
- Taiwan's Taiex stock index climbed 1.7% to a record close following Nvidia's announcement of a $150 billion spending plan in Taiwan (per CNBC).
- Micron Technology shares surged nearly 20% after reporting strong earnings, contributing to a broader rally in semiconductor stocks (per CNBC).

## Federal Reserve & Monetary Policy
- Kevin Warsh was sworn in as the new chairman of the Federal Reserve, with expectations of a "regime change" in monetary policy management (per Federal Reserve Press Releases).
- The Fed's favorite inflation indicator, the PCE Price Index, is set to be released tomorrow, with recent reports indicating inflation is elevated (per Yahoo Finance).

## Corporate Earnings & News
- JPMorgan Chase CEO Jamie Dimon indicated the bank could spend up to $20 billion on acquisitions, marking a significant potential deal in the banking sector (per CNBC).
- Zscaler shares fell over 23% after the company provided a revenue forecast below analyst expectations, despite beating earnings estimates (per CNBC).
- Workday shares rose over 8% after reporting stronger-than-expected results and raising its full-year margin outlook (per CNBC).

## Cryptocurrency & Digital Assets
- Bitcoin opened at $75,829.41, down 1.9%, while Ethereum also saw a similar decline, reflecting ongoing market pressures (per Yahoo Finance).
- Cathie Wood of ARK Invest raised her five-year Bitcoin price prediction to $1.25 million, citing accelerating institutional adoption (per Yahoo Finance).
- Robinhood introduced AI trading features, allowing users to automate stock trading and purchases (per CoinDesk).

## Key Themes of the Day
- Geopolitical tensions, particularly regarding the U.S.-Iran conflict, continue to influence oil prices and market sentiment.
- The rise of AI in finance and trading is reshaping investment strategies and corporate operations.
- Semiconductor stocks are gaining traction amid significant investments from major players like Nvidia.

## Market Sentiment
- Mixed sentiment persists in the market, with optimism surrounding tech and semiconductor stocks contrasted by concerns over geopolitical tensions and inflation.
- Traders remain skeptical about the timeline for normalization in oil supply through the Strait of Hormuz, impacting energy market forecasts (per CNBC).
